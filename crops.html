Crops Grown in India

India grows a wide variety of crops â€” the following list highlights major staples and cash crops with quick facts and typical growing regions.

Rice

Rice is the primary staple for much of eastern, southern and northeastern India. Popular varieties include Basmati, Sona Masuri and IR varieties. Requires standing water during early stages.

Regions: West Bengal, Uttar Pradesh, Punjab (irrigated), Assam.

Wheat

Primary rabi (winter) cereal with major production in the Indo-Gangetic plains. Best grown with adequate cold period and timely irrigation.

Regions: Punjab, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh.

Maize

Versatile cereal used for human food, animal feed and industry. Grown during Kharif and rabi in many states with varying inputs.

Regions: Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Bihar, Madhya Pradesh.

Sugarcane

High water-demand cash crop grown for sugar and jaggery production. Often grown on irrigated plains and requiring long growing periods.

Regions: Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, Karnataka.

Cotton

Key fiber crop supporting India's textile industry. Grown in diverse zones; hybrid and Bt varieties are widely used to manage pests.

Regions: Maharashtra, Gujarat, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh.

Tomato

Tomato is an important vegetable crop grown across India in multiple seasons. It requires warm days, cool nights and careful pest management to maintain quality.

Regions: Maharashtra, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Uttar Pradesh.
