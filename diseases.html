Common Crop Diseases

Late Blight (Potato, Tomato)

Caused by Phytophthora infestans, it thrives in cool, wet conditions and can cause rapid crop loss. Control measures include resistant varieties, removing infected material, and timely fungicide application where necessary.

Blast (Rice)

Rice blast (Magnaporthe oryzae) affects leaves, nodes and panicles and is influenced by humidity and nitrogen levels. Managing fertilizer, using tolerant varieties and field hygiene help reduce outbreaks.

Wheat Rusts

Stem, leaf and stripe rusts (caused by Puccinia spp.) are major constraints; resistant cultivars, monitoring and fungicide sprays are common management options.

Bacterial Spot & Blight

Bacterial diseases (e.g., Xanthomonas spp.) cause leaf spots, blights and fruit lesions in many vegetables and fruit crops. Good sanitation, certified seed/seedlings and copper-based sprays where appropriate can help manage outbreaks.

Bacterial Wilt

Caused by Ralstonia solanacearum and related bacteria; leads to rapid wilting and plant death, especially in solanaceous crops. Use resistant varieties, crop rotation and clean planting material to reduce risk.

Viral Diseases (Mosaic)

Viruses such as Tobacco Mosaic Virus and others cause mosaic patterns, stunting and reduced yields. Control relies on resistant varieties, vector (insect) management and removing infected plants.

Fusarium Wilt

Fusarium oxysporum causes vascular wilts in many crops (tomato, banana etc.). Use tolerant cultivars, soil solarization and crop rotation to manage soil-borne inoculum.

Nematode Damage (Root-knot)

Root-knot nematodes (Meloidogyne spp.) cause galls on roots, poor growth and yield loss. Management includes resistant varieties, crop rotation, organic amendments and, where permitted, nematicides.
