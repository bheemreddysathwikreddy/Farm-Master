Common Crop Diseases

Early identification and integrated management reduce losses — use resistant varieties, crop rotation, timely fungicide and good field sanitation.

Late Blight (Potato, Tomato)

Caused by Phytophthora infestans, it thrives in cool, wet conditions and can cause rapid crop loss. Control measures include resistant varieties, removing infected material, and timely fungicide application.

Blast (Rice)

Rice blast (Magnaporthe oryzae) affects leaves, nodes and panicles. Managing fertilizer, using tolerant varieties and field hygiene help reduce outbreaks.

Wheat Rusts

Stem, leaf and stripe rusts (Puccinia spp.) are major constraints. Resistant cultivars, monitoring and fungicide sprays are common controls.

Bacterial Spot & Blight

Bacterial diseases (Xanthomonas spp.) cause leaf spots and blights. Sanitation, certified seed and copper-based sprays help manage outbreaks.

Bacterial Wilt

Caused by Ralstonia solanacearum, leading to rapid wilting. Resistant varieties and crop rotation reduce risk.

Viral Diseases (Mosaic)

Viruses like Tobacco Mosaic Virus cause mosaic patterns and stunting. Control relies on resistant varieties and insect vector management.

Fusarium Wilt

Fusarium oxysporum causes vascular wilts in crops like tomato and banana. Use tolerant cultivars, soil solarization and crop rotation.

Nematode Damage (Root-knot)

Root-knot nematodes cause galls on roots and yield loss. Management includes resistant varieties and organic amendments.

Powdery Mildew

Powdery mildew appears as white powdery growth on leaves and stems. It affects cereals, vegetables and fruit crops. Control includes resistant varieties, proper spacing, and sulfur or fungicide sprays.
